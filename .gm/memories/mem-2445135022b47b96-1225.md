CONFIRMED by same-pose A/B ON the NVIDIA machine (RTX 3060, ANGLE D3D11): a cross-size DEPTH blitFramebuffer (full-res scene FBO -> half-res overlay FBO depth, NEAREST downscale) produces WRONG depth on NVIDIA/ANGLE/D3D11 -- it rejects MOST of the half-res transparent overlay (only a near/deep region survives), so 'only some water draws'. Toggling the depth blit OFF (no depth test) restored the full overlay at the exact same pose = the blit is the sole culprit; AMD/ANGLE tolerated the same blit. This is a DRIVER/translator bug class, not your logic. THE FIX: never depth-blit between mismatched FBO sizes for occlusion -- give the scene FBO a sampleable DEPTH TEXTURE and do occlusion in the overlay FS (reconstruct/compare the overlay fragment's depth against the sampled full-res scene depth, discard if behind). Per-pixel accurate, no blit, portable. METHOD WIN: the bug was invisible until the user switched the browser spooler to the failing GPU -- always get a witness surface ON the failing vendor before trusting a fix; a vendor-specific artifact you can't see on your GPU is GL undefined-behaviour/driver divergence, and same-pose A/B of each sub-operation (here: depth-blit on/off) pins it in two screenshots.
