WATER SURFACE LOOK (refraction/transparency/scatter) must derive from PURE VIEW GEOMETRY (view-ray distance + grazing angle 1/NoV), NEVER from the interpolated seabed ELEVATION varying. Keying opacity off coarse interpolated per-vertex depth produces per-mesh-cell 'windows' that look wrong at an angle (user hard rule). The recurring white/milky-sheet defect on water at grazing angles is the bright-SKY REFLECTION (Fresnel pow(1-NoV,5)->1 at grazing): the fix is to CAP the reflection weight low (<=~0.12) and TINT the reflected colour toward the water body colour so a full Fresnel reflection is sea-blue, not white -- and do NOT weight reflection UP by optical depth/path (that makes grazing water MORE reflective = more milk). Also avoid grazing 1/NoV AMPLIFICATION of the scatter optical path for CLOSE water -- it drives near grazing water to full-scatter milk; use straight view distance for the scatter e-fold so close water stays clear/refractive at any angle. General lesson: a screen-space material driven by an interpolated geometry varying steps per-triangle and reads as faceted windows; drive it from continuous view-ray geometry instead.
